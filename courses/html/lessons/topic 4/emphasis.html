4.1 Importance vs Emphasis

The Voice of the Document: Controlling semantic inflection and urgency.

Visuals vs. Semantics

In the early days of the web, we used tags purely for visual styling: <b> for bold and <i> for italics.

Modern HTML5 separates Presentation (CSS) from Meaning (HTML). As Architects, we choose tags based on the inflection we want the browser or screen reader to apply, not the font style.

The Stress Emphasis: <em>

The <em> element represents stress emphasis. It changes the nuance of a sentence. Ideally, a screen reader will verbally stress this word.

Consider the sentence "I love coding." Shifting the emphasis changes the meaning entirely:

  • "I love coding" (Me, not you).
  • "I love coding" (A strong feeling).
  • "I love coding" (As opposed to designing).

The Strong Importance: <strong>

The <strong> element implies strong importance, seriousness, or urgency. It is not just "bold text." It is a warning or a key keyword that must not be skipped.

Nesting strong elements increases their relative importance.

The Survival of <b> and <i>

Are <b> and <i> dead? No. HTML5 redefined them for utilitarian purposes where no extra semantic importance is implied.

Tag Old Usage New HTML5 Definition
<b> Bold text Keywords, product names, or stylistic offsets without importance.
<i> Italic text Taxonomic designations, technical terms, idiomatic phrases (e.g., de facto), or icons.
