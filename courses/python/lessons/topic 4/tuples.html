4.4 Tuples (Immutable)

A Tuple is like a list, but with one major difference: it is Immutable (cannot be changed after creation).

Syntax

Tuples are written with round brackets ().

coordinates = (10, 20)
dimensions = (1920, 1080)

Why use Tuples?

  • Safety: Data cannot be accidentally modified.
  • Performance: Tuples are slightly faster than lists in Python.
  • Constant Data: Perfect for things like days of the week or fixed settings.
Warning: coordinates[0] = 5 will raise a TypeError. You cannot reassign tuple values.
